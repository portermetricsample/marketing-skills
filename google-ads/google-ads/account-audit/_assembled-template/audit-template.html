Executive summary

The account looks more efficient than it really is — because the conversion signal got noisier, not because the buying got smarter.

Over the last 90 days Acme Insurance spent $605K and CPA fell 32% ($450 → $306) — which reads like a big win. But conversions jumped 76% while conversion value rose only 16%, so the value of an average "conversion" dropped 34% and ROAS actually slipped to 1.87×. Translation: Google is being told to chase a larger pile of shallower events (page views, app-starts, deprecated Universal Analytics goals) that are still flagged as primary conversions.

Where it leaks: the conversion definition (153 actions, dozens of shallow ones counted as primary). The biggest single campaign, Life_SEM ($252K), is also losing 38% of impressions to Ad Rank, not budget — a quality problem, not a money problem.

The good news: the fundamentals the team usually worries about are clean — location is set to Presence (not "presence or interest"), Search Partners and the Display Network are off on Search, offline/CRM conversions are importing, and the landing pages are genuinely strong.

Do first: clean the conversion signal (keep only down-funnel actions as primary) and move budget into the two proven campaigns that are capped (Health and Life-BroadMatch). Those moves touch the efficiency of nearly the whole $605K.

01

Conversion tracking & what bidding optimizes toward

● High Broken

Offline import works — the problem is the account counts too many things as a conversion. Of 153 conversion actions, dozens of shallow ones (page views, app-starts, and sunset Universal Analytics goals) are still enabled and marked primary, so Smart Bidding optimizes toward them alongside real revenue. That is the most likely reason conversions rose 76% while value rose only 16%.

What we checkedStateEvidenceRecommendation (where · what · why)
Offline / CRM import in place? OK Multiple enabled UPLOAD_CLICKS actions: "All/HD/TLI/CI Payment Received SSR", "Policy Approved/Create SSR", "App Start SSR" + a Qualified-Lead upload. Keep. Down-funnel CRM events (policy approved, payment received) are importing — this is the right backbone. No change.
Are "primary" conversions actually down-funnel? Broken Enabled + primary page-view actions feeding bidding: "Life - Submit Application (Master View)", "Life - Start Application Page View (USE THIS)", "Life - Application Page View", "Critical Illness - Start App Page View", "Quotes Standalone Continued (Analytics)". Mixed in with real PURCHASE / Policy-Approved actions. Where: Account → Goals → Conversions. What: set only down-funnel actions (Policy Approved, Payment Received, Qualified Lead) as Primary; switch every page-view / app-start action to Secondary. Why: bidding currently can't tell a page view from a sold policy, so it buys cheap shallow events and inflates the conversion count.
Deprecated Universal Analytics goals removed? Review Several enabled UNIVERSAL_ANALYTICS_GOAL actions (e.g. "Life - Recommendation Page View (Analytics)", "Critical Illness - App Full Address Page View (Master View)", "Quotes Standalone Continued (Analytics)"). UA was shut down July 2024. Where: the UA-type conversion actions. What: archive/remove them (they no longer receive real data). Why: stale goals clutter the account and risk being re-counted.
Do primary actions carry a value? Broken Enabled, primary, value $0: "Lead-form Start A" — 292 conv, $0; "Lead-form Start B" — 22 conv, $0. Meanwhile Life runs on value-based bidding. Where: the $0-value primary actions. What: either assign a realistic value (e.g. expected policy value × close-rate) or demote them to Secondary. Why: a $0 conversion tells value-bidding the click was worthless, starving good campaigns of budget.
Fix
One change fixes most of this: a clean primary set of down-funnel events (Policy Approved / Payment Received / Qualified Lead) with values attached. Expect the conversion count to drop and CPA to rise on paper — that's the signal getting honest, and bidding will start steering toward dollars instead of page views.

Next, the bid targets — do they match reality? They do, once you read them from the right source.

Why no number
Quality Score is a summing metric in this dataset — the same keyword text across multiple ad groups adds up past 10, so a raw "QS = 28" is meaningless. Google's three pillars (Ad Relevance, Expected CTR, Landing Page Experience) are reliable at any grain, so the audit grades on those.
Ad copy
We read the live ads — the copy is strong. Every top ad group runs 10–15 headlines including "Coverage From $1/Day", "Qualify Without A Medical Test", "Same-day coverage" and "Apply In 15 Minutes". Nearly all assets are still PENDING (Google hasn't graded them) — and critically, the high-spend ad groups under review (Best Life Consolidated, Life Phrase & Exact, Term, Dental, Health) have no graded assets yet, so you can't prune "low" or lean on "best" there. (Only the Brand and Life-BroadMatch "Affordable" groups already show a few GOOD/LEARNING assets.) So the relevance gap is structural (consolidated ad group + broad-match keyword/location insertion), not missing copy. Action: split by intent, fill descriptions to 4, then revisit asset ratings in ~30 days.

Ad Rank loss on these terms ties directly to where the budget is — and isn't — going.
